Privacy & GDPR

Privacy and data protection information

The Office of Alternate Academia is a fictional and satirical web publication. This website is intended for informational, artistic, and entertainment purposes.

1. Personal data collection

This website does not intentionally collect personal data from visitors. It does not provide account registration, comment functionality, newsletter subscriptions, or contact forms at this time.

2. Cookies and tracking

This website does not use advertising trackers, profiling technologies, or analytics systems for behavioural marketing purposes. No attempt is made to identify visitors individually.

3. Embedded third-party content

Some articles may include embedded content from third-party platforms such as YouTube or Instagram. These services may place cookies or process technical data in accordance with their own privacy policies when the embedded content is loaded.

Visitors are encouraged to consult the privacy documentation of those third-party services if they interact with embedded media.

4. Hosting and technical logs

Like most websites, this site may be delivered through hosting infrastructure that generates basic technical logs, such as server requests, browser type, or general diagnostic information. Such technical processing is handled by the relevant hosting provider and is not used here for personalised tracking.

5. GDPR principles

This website is designed with data minimisation in mind. Since it does not intentionally collect or profile personal data, its operation follows the general spirit of privacy-first and GDPR-aligned publishing: collecting as little information as possible and avoiding unnecessary intrusion into the visitor experience.

6. Changes to this page

This privacy notice may be updated in the future if the website introduces new features, such as contact forms, mailing lists, or analytics tools.

7. Contact

If official contact details are added in the future, they will be published on this website. Until then, all decrees remain non-binding, and all surveillance committees remain fictional.
